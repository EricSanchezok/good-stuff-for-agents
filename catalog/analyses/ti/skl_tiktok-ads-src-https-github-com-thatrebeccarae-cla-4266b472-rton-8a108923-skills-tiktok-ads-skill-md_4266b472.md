# TikTok Ads

This is a TikTok advertising platform reference card that does one thing the other ad-platform skills in this collection don't: it actually teaches creative strategy. The TikTok Creative Formula section ("Hook → Value/Story → CTA") is concrete, operational, and tied to platform-specific behaviors that can't be derived from general marketing knowledge. But the rest of the skill is a feature catalog, and the gap between the creative section and everything else is wide enough to drive a campaign budget through.

## Why it matters

The creative formula is the skill's real contribution. "Hook in first 3 seconds — use text overlay, unexpected visual, or direct address," "Native aesthetic — look like a TikTok, not an ad," "Cut every 2-3 seconds — static shots die." These are not generic marketing tips. They are TikTok-specific production rules that would change how someone shoots, edits, and scripts their ads. The claim that creative fatigue sets in 2-3x faster on TikTok than Meta is a specific, actionable insight that justifies a different refresh cadence than the other platforms.

Compare this to the campaign objectives and ad formats sections, which are essentially TikTok Business Center documentation translated into a markdown table. The TopView ad format description ("Full-screen on app open, up to 60s") is accurate but adds nothing a competent agent couldn't retrieve from TikTok's own docs.

The audit checklist is slightly better than LinkedIn's — it has 10 items with specific thresholds like "Budget allows 50+ conversions per week per ad group (for learning phase)" and "Creative refreshed within last 14 days." These are real operational checks. But unlike the Facebook Ads skill, there are no hard rules with violation consequences, no scored audit, and no tie to a scoring system. The checklist is a checklist, not a diagnostic framework.

One notable absence: the skill says "TikTok Shop" and "Live Shopping" exist as ad formats but provides zero guidance on how to run them. Social commerce is TikTok's biggest differentiator from Meta and Google, and this skill treats it as a checkbox.

## Where it helps, where it hurts

**Best case**: A DTC ecommerce brand that already runs Meta Ads and is expanding to TikTok. They need to understand what's different — faster creative fatigue, native aesthetic requirements, sound-on consumption, Spark Ads as an authenticity play. This skill gives them exactly that: the platform-specific creative rules that would otherwise take 3-6 months of trial and error to learn. Combined with the benchmarks table, it sets realistic expectations for CPMs ($8-15 average) and conversion rates (1-2%).

**Worst case**: A brand with no video production capability asks the agent to "set up TikTok Ads." The skill will confidently describe the creative formula — hook, story, CTA, 9:16 vertical, sound on, cut every 2-3 seconds — but has zero guidance on how to actually produce that creative. There's no script template, no brief format, no guidance on working with creators. The agent will deliver a creative strategy document that the brand can't execute, which is worse than no strategy at all because it creates the illusion of readiness.

The skill is also silent on TikTok's moderation and ad policy quirks, which are meaningfully different from Meta's. Certain claims, before/after images, and financial language that pass on Meta will get rejected on TikTok. A user following this skill's creative advice could produce ads that TikTok rejects for policy violations the skill never mentions.

## What it quietly assumes

The biggest hidden assumption is that the user can produce short-form vertical video at speed. The refresh cadence recommendation — every 7-14 days, 3-5 active creatives per ad group — implies a production velocity of roughly 8-20 new video ads per month. That requires either an in-house video team, a creator partnership program, or a UGC sourcing pipeline. None of these are mentioned. The skill assumes the creative supply chain exists.

It also assumes the user's product works visually. TikTok is a visual-first platform. A B2B SaaS product, a financial service, or an enterprise tool that doesn't demo well in 15-30 second vertical video will get none of the benefit this skill promises. The skill never asks "does your product work on TikTok?" — it assumes it does.

The lookalike audience section mentions a minimum of 1,000 users in the seed audience. That's accurate but dangerously low — lookalikes from a 1,000-user seed will perform poorly. The skill doesn't flag that lookalike quality scales with seed size, and that 1,000-user seeds produce audiences that are barely better than broad targeting.

## What could go wrong

The primary risk is creative burnout disguised as a strategy document. The skill makes TikTok advertising sound like a creative formula you apply, but the reality is that TikTok's algorithm rewards creative diversity far more than any targeting optimization. An agent following this skill could produce an audit that emphasizes audience overlap and budget allocation while ignoring the actual bottleneck — not enough good creative. The skill's own audit checklist puts "each ad group has 3-5 active creatives" as the second item, but the template provides no diagnostic for when that creative isn't working.

The "Spark Ads" recommendation carries a specific risk: boosting creator content requires proper whitelisting and permissions through TikTok's authorization flow. If the user doesn't have this set up, the agent could recommend a campaign type that can't be executed. The skill mentions Spark Ads exist but provides no setup instructions.

As with the other skills in this collection, there are no tool-based risks — the skill can't access TikTok Ads Manager. The worst outcome is bad advice that wastes creative production resources and ad spend on a platform where the user's product or production capability was never a good fit.

## Bottom line

Pick this skill if you specifically need TikTok creative strategy guidance and already know how to run paid social campaigns — the creative formula and fatigue insights are genuine and platform-specific. Skip it for anything involving campaign setup, TikTok Shop, or policy navigation. The biggest risk is recommending a creative velocity the user can't sustain. The biggest benefit is the creative formula, which encodes 12-18 months of trial-and-error learning into seven bullet points. In a 100-skill catalog, this earns a spot only if there's no stronger TikTok Ads skill available — it has one excellent section and a lot of filler.
